About

Me

My long journey to become a software developer has taken many paths. I was born in San Juan, Puerto Rico, and was raised in a small town called Winchester in Virginia. My family was usually a part of the early majority when it came to owning new technology. Eager to understand and work with computers, my three brothers and I dabbled into coding at a young age. They are much older than I, and all three of them went into computer science. I found love in the music industry and shifted my focus into business and entertainment. I graduated high school in 2012, and earned a bachelors of science in Marketing in 2015. I then moved to Philadelphia and was primairly a touring musician for a few years. That all came to an end in March 2020 when the Covid-19 pandemic began. With my new free time, I got back into my roots and rediscovered my love for programming. I felt a strong urge to dive deeper and make a career change. I enrolled into the coding bootcamp at University of Richmond in May 2021.
